Sources of support

  • Your supervisor(s)
    • They have done this before, though perhaps a while ago; they may engage to varying degrees with you in the job search process
  • Your peers in the PhD programme
    • They are in the same situation you are in - commiseration, sharing resources, tips etc.
  • Career resources from Uni, elsewhere
    • Generally very good, more expertise than academics
  • Family and Friends
    • Emotional support

Planning - explicit

1 Strategy about what to apply for and when

  • Consider your constraints, what is a must-have for you in a job?

    • e.g., located in X; research-focused job, etc.
    • these can change as you change!

  • Consider your desirables, what do you want from a job?

  • General advice: cast a wide net with applications, but only for jobs that fit your must-have criteria

Planning - explicit

2 Marketing yourself

Though this will depend on the nature of the job; broadly, this means

  • Talking about your research in way that is accessible and makes a case for your qualificaions
  • Identifying and emphasizing other skills that you have which are relevant to the job
  • Making connections between what you offer and what they want

Planning - explicit

3 putting together application materials and interview prep

  • Research statement
  • Letters of recommendation
  • CV
  • Cover letter
  • Preparing for interviews
    • researching the job, employer, department
    • mock interviews with colleagues/supervisors
    • doing interviews

Implicit planning, and the “hidden cirriculum”

IMO - one of the best things you can do to prepare for employment is to make the most of your PhD time and build skills organically

(Easier said than done, esp.given the time constraints)

  • Doing research and writing thesis
  • Tutoring
  • Other transferable skills not directly related to thesis
  • Publishing papers, when possible
  • Building connections with other researchers

Panel Questions

  1. What is one thing you wish you had known before going on the job market?
  2. What did you do to prepare for going on the job market prior to submitting any applications?
  3. What sources of support/guidance did you make use of when applying for jobs?
  4. What are some specific things that you did during your PhD which helped you be successful on the job market?
